A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Sir

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

He played a crucial role in the development of India's space and missile programs, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India".

Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate of science and education. He was deeply admired for his simplicity, humility, and his inspirational speeches, especially among the youth.

Among his notable achievements, Dr. Kalam played a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, and the successful launch of the Indian Space Research Organisation's (ISRO) Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV).

Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and honorary doctorates from over 40 universities worldwide.
